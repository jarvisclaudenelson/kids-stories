# Chapter 9: The Secret Signal

Ezra woke up late the next morning. The sun was high in the sky. He had been very tired after visiting the crystal spire.

Julian was sitting on his bed. He was eating a big, crunchy apple. 

"You slept for a long time!" Julian said. 

Ezra touched his chest. He felt the warm glow of his fragment. It was safe. 

"Did I miss anything?" Ezra asked. 

"Declan made you something," Julian said. He pointed to a small table.

On the table was a messy drawing. It was purple and blue. 

"What is it?" Ezra asked. 

"It is a picture of you and Ollie," Julian explained. "Declan thinks you are purple. He just really likes the purple crayon."

Ezra laughed. He liked the picture. He felt lucky to have such a funny family.

---

Later that day, Sable came into the room. She looked serious. 

"We are going to the relay station," she said. "Captain Okonkwo wants us to try something."

Ezra hopped out of bed. "What are we doing?"

"We are going to use your fragment to send a message," Sable said. "We want to see if anyone else is out there."

Julian grabbed his notebook. He loved taking notes. 

"I have ten questions!" Julian shouted. 

"Save them for the walk," Sable said with a tiny smile. 

Ezra felt a little nervous. He had never sent a message with his star before. What if it didn't work?

---

Everyone got ready to go. But then, little Declan stood by the door. 

He was wearing his tiny blue coat and his brown boots. He was holding his toy dog. 

"I do it!" Declan said. 

"No, Declan," Mom said. "You have to stay here with me."

Declan shook his head. He looked very determined. 

"I do it!" he said again. He pointed at Ezra. 

Declan wanted to help his big brother. He didn't want to be left behind. 

In the end, Mom agreed to walk with him to the center of the colony. Declan was happy. He marched down the path like a little soldier. 

"He is very brave for a two-year-old," Julian whispered. 

---

The relay station was a tall metal building on a hill. It had a giant antenna that pointed at the stars. 

Inside, there were lots of computers and blinking lights. It smelled like old metal and electricity. 

Captain Okonkwo was waiting for them. 

"Okay, Ezra," she said. "Stand in the middle of the room. When we turn on the machine, try to reach out with your fragment."

Ezra stood very still. He closed his eyes. 

"Ready?" a lady named Pella asked. She was the engineer. 

"Ready," Ezra said. 

Pella pushed a big button. The computers started to hum. 

---

The room felt buzzy. Ezra focused on the star in his chest. 

He didn't try to push. He just stayed quiet and listened. 

Suddenly, Ollie the dog went very still. His ears perked up. 

Ezra felt a tiny tug in his chest. It wasn't a sound. It was more like a feeling. 

It felt cold and dark. And very, very lonely. 

"Someone is there," Ezra whispered. 

He could feel another fragment far away. It was calling for help. It sounded tired, like it had been waiting for a long time. 

"They are at a place called Bryn Dural," Captain Okonkwo said. She looked at a screen. "That is a long way from here."

---

Ezra opened his eyes. He felt tired but also excited. 

"They are all alone," Ezra told the adults. "We have to go find them."

Sable nodded. She looked at her own pendant. 

"There are seven fragments in total," Sable reminded them. "The Covenant put them in different places. We need to find all of them to save the world."

Julian wrote everything down in his notebook. 

"This is going to be a big adventure," Julian said. 

"A scary one," Ezra added. 

But then he thought of Declan. If Declan could be brave, so could he. 

"When do we leave?" Ezra asked.

---

That night, the family sat around the dinner table. They ate soup and bread. 

Declan wanted to hear all about the relay station. 

"Lone-ee?" Declan asked. 

"Yes, Declan," Ezra said. "The person far away is very lonely."

Declan patted Ezra's hand. "Hug?" 

"We will give them a hug when we find them," Ezra promised. 

Ollie wagged his tail under the table. 

Outside, the stars were shining bright. Ezra knew that somewhere out there, a new friend was waiting for them. 

He wasn't just a boy anymore. He was a hero on a mission.
