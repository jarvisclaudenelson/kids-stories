# Chapter 11: The Shadow Hand's Secret

When the *Ariadne* returned to Monscollis, everyone was waiting. 

Rafe was a little shy at first. But then Declan ran up to him. 

"Rafe!" Declan shouted. He patted Rafe's knee. Then he handed him a piece of toast. "Eat!"

Rafe laughed. "Thank you, Declan."

Ezra smiled. Declan always knew how to make new friends feel at home. 

---

That afternoon, Captain Okonkwo called a meeting. 

"We have some big news," she said. "Dr. Fletcher told us what the Shadow Hand wants."

Ezra leaned in. "What do they want?"

"They don't want to use the fragments," the Captain said. "They want to *shatter* them. They want to break them into tiny pieces so no one can ever use the power again."

"But that would be terrible!" Julian said. "The world needs the ancient power to stay safe."

"They are afraid," Sable explained. "They think the power is too dangerous. They want to make it go away forever."

---

Ezra felt a chill. The Shadow Hand was even scarier than he thought. 

"How many do they need to break them?" Ezra asked. 

"Five," the Captain said. "If they get five fragments, they can break all of them. They already have two."

"And we have three," Julian said, counting on his fingers. "That means they only need three more to win."

"Then we have to find the last two first," Ezra said. He looked at his glowing star. He wouldn't let anyone break it. 

---

Later, Ezra and Rafe went to the forest to train. 

Ollie ran around the trees, sniffing for any "coldie mans." He was a very good guard dog. 

"Watch this," Ezra said. He focused on his arm. It stretched out five feet, then six, then ten! 

"Whoa!" Rafe said. "That is so cool!"

Then it was Rafe's turn. He looked at a heavy branch on the ground. He didn't move a muscle, but the branch lifted into the air. 

"We are like a team of superheroes," Ezra said. 

---

Sable watched them train. She looked happy for the first time in a long while. 

"You are both doing great," she said. "But the Shadow Hand has a secret weapon. They have a 'Pattern Reader'."

"What's that?" Julian asked, looking up from his notebook. 

"It is a person who knows where all the fragments are," Sable said. "They have a map that shows every piece."

"Then we need a better map," Julian said. He started drawing lines in his notebook. "I'm going to figure out the pattern myself!"

---

That night, Declan was helping Rafe get settled in. 

Rafe was staying in the extra bunk in the boys' room. 

"Too loud," Declan said, even though the room was quiet. He tucked a small blanket around Rafe's feet. 

"Thank you, Decker," Rafe said. 

Ezra looked out the window at the crystal spire. It was pulsing like a heartbeat. 

"We are going to protect the fragments," Ezra whispered. 

"And each other," Rafe added. 

Ezra felt brave. They were a family now, and nothing could break a family apart.
