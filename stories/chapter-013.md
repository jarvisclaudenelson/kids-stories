# Chapter 13: HD 34729

The Ariadne dropped out of jump at 0312 ship-time, and the planet was right where Sable's chart said it would be.

It had no name. Just the catalog number, HD 34729-c — third body around a yellow-orange star that looked a lot like the one Monscollis orbited, only older. More settled. The planet below was blue-grey with green at the poles, cloud cover thin enough to see through in places. A survey runner had swept it twice, concluded it had water and stable pressure and nothing worth colonizing, and left.

Someone had stayed.

Or rather, someone had ended up here — which was different.

Wren brought them in without comment, which was how Wren did everything. She had a way of making landing on an unnamed planet feel like pulling into a familiar parking spot. Ezra watched her hands on the controls and thought she and Sable had more in common than either of them would ever admit.

"Signal?" Rafe asked.

Ezra pressed his hand to his chest. The star was bright and active, the way it got in the last few minutes before something significant. Not threat. Not alarm. Something more like *almost.*

"Close," he said. "And pointing at us."

---

The fragment had been reaching out for three hours. Now that the Ariadne was in atmosphere, Ezra could feel the signal the way you feel a voice when you've finally gotten to the room it's coming from.

Sharp. Present. Careful — but barely, now. The careful was loosening.

Whatever was at the surface had been holding itself together for a long time.

Wren found a flat stretch of grey rock and set the Ariadne down clean. The landing struts sank half an inch into softer ground beneath the rock layer. She checked it, frowned slightly, and announced: "Stable."

Marcus had the door before she finished saying it.

Outside air came in — cool, heavier than Monscollis, with a smell like rain that happened three days ago. The sky was low and the color of old pewter. One sun, not two. Two of HD 34729's four moons were visible at the horizon, pale and close together.

Ollie jumped out first. He put his nose down immediately — sweeping, systematic — then looked up in one direction and held.

"That way," Julian said.

"Yes," Ezra said. The star agreed.

---

They found her on a ridge about four hundred meters from the landing site.

She was sitting on a flat rock with her knees up and her arms around them, watching the Ariadne settle. She had red hair, shorter than Rafe's, cropped close at the sides. Pale skin that had gone weather-tanned. Practical clothes — not colony-issue, older. Like she'd been stretching the same set of gear past its intended use.

She was maybe eleven. Maybe twelve.

Around her neck, on a simple cord, was a stone that glowed a color Ezra didn't have a word for. Not quite violet. Not quite white. Something in between that shifted depending on the angle — like the inside of a shell, iridescent, alive.

She looked at the three of them — Ezra, Sable, Rafe — and at the light happening at their chests in response to her stone, and her expression did something complicated.

She had clearly been waiting for this. She had clearly been afraid it would never happen.

"Hi," Ezra said.

She said: "You're shorter than I expected."

---

Her name was Petra.

She'd been on HD 34729 for eleven months — seven months before the fragment, four months after. She'd come with her uncle's survey team, which had also turned out badly for everyone involved, mostly because the survey team had included a Shadow Hand procurement agent who nobody had identified until he tried to take the fragment out of her hands and she'd run.

"I didn't even know what it was," she said. She was walking with them back toward the Ariadne, Ollie at her knee like they'd known each other for years. "I found it in the drill core sample and it just — it was warm. Everything else on this planet is cold. So I put it in my pocket."

"And the agent?" Marcus asked.

"He chased me for two days. Then he stopped." She looked at her hands. "I think I scared him."

"What did you do?" Rafe asked.

"I didn't mean to do it. I was cornered in one of the survey outbuildings and he was blocking the door and I was very, very angry—" She paused. "And the whole wall lit up."

Julian, who had been quietly walking and not asking questions (unusual), said: "What color?"

Petra looked at him sideways. "Purple-white. Why?"

"Just tracking." He made a note.

She watched him do this. "Who are you?"

"Julian. Ezra's brother."

"The analyst."

Julian looked up sharply. "How did you know that?"

"I've been feeling you through the fragments for three months," she said simply. "There's a very particular feeling when someone is analyzing something. It has angles."

Julian stared at her for a long moment. Then he wrote something else down.

---

Inside the Ariadne, the three glows — gold, amber, silver-blue — met a fourth.

Petra kept the fragment on its cord, and when she sat across from Ezra and Rafe and Sable and stopped keeping it tucked under her collar, the iridescent violet-white light found the others.

The chord changed.

Ezra had felt the two-note chord — amber and gold. He'd felt the three-note chord form at the crystal spire when Rafe's fragment synced with his. What happened now was something he didn't have a word for.

Four notes. Different pitches, different temperatures — warm gold, warm amber, cool silver-blue, and now this: something clear and strange that felt like the feeling you get when you look at something impossible and realize it's actually real.

It didn't feel like music exactly. More like what music was made of before it became sound.

Sable had her hand around her pendant. Her eyes were closed. "I can feel it this time," she said quietly. "Not just the change. The chord itself."

Rafe's amber fragment had gone very bright. He looked at it, surprised.

"Is this normal?" he asked.

"Nothing about this is normal," Sable said. "That's not a useful question anymore."

"What's a useful question?"

She opened her eyes. "What does yours feel like when it's near hers."

Rafe thought about it. "Like it's standing up straighter."

Petra looked at him. "Mine too." She had the particular expression of someone who has been alone with something strange for a long time and is only now finding out there was a name for it.

---

It took an hour and a half to get the full story.

Petra had grown up on survey ships — her uncle was a geologist who did contract work for three different colony consortiums, which meant she'd had more addresses by age eleven than most adults had by fifty. She'd been twelve when they landed on HD 34729, thirteen when everything went wrong.

She'd been here alone, essentially, for four months. The Shadow Hand agent had fled after the wall incident. What remained of the survey crew had evacuated in the emergency runner — she hadn't been at the landing site when they left, and by the time she got there, they were gone.

She didn't sound bitter about it. She sounded like someone who had made peace with a fact that still hurt.

"I found the fragment in month seven," she said. "Before that I was just surviving. After—" She shrugged. "It helped. It's hard to explain."

"You don't have to explain it," Ezra said.

"No, I want to." She turned the stone over in her fingers. "Before the fragment, the planet felt empty. After, I could feel... something. Like background music you can almost hear. Not lonely exactly. Just—" She looked at the viewport, at the grey-green planet outside. "Like something knew I was here. And didn't mind."

Nobody filled the silence after that. It was the kind of thing that shouldn't be filled.

Declan would have asked if she wanted toast. Ezra thought about that and had to look at the floor for a second.

---

The funny thing was the name.

They'd been on the planet for about forty minutes when Petra mentioned, offhandedly, that she'd started calling it something.

"Called what?" Rafe asked.

"The planet." She looked a little embarrassed. "I know it's just a catalog number. But I was here for eleven months and I needed to call it something."

"What did you call it?" Julian asked.

"Gerald."

Silence.

"Gerald," Sable said.

"I needed a name that felt dependable," Petra said. "Gerald felt dependable."

Julian wrote it down with the specific expression of someone who wants to ask a follow-up question but is selecting carefully. "Why not something like... Luminos. Or Vela. Or—"

"Those sound like they're trying too hard," Petra said firmly. "Gerald doesn't try too hard."

Ollie walked over and put his head in her lap with a sigh.

"See?" she said. "Ollie gets it."

Rafe looked at Julian. Julian looked at his notebook. He had written *Gerald* and underlined it.

"Fine," Julian said. "Gerald."

Marcus, who had not said a word during any of this, made a very quiet sound that was definitely a laugh that he immediately converted into a cough.

---

They had to go quickly — the window was less than an hour now.

Petra packed in eight minutes. This was faster than anyone else on the Ariadne had ever packed, including Wren, which said something.

She had one bag. It wasn't big. When Ezra asked what was in it, she said: "Survey tools, three books, the fragment, and a piece of rock from Gerald that I took on day one."

"Sentimental," Rafe said.

"I was here for eleven months," she said. "I'm allowed one rock."

"You're allowed many rocks," Sable said. "You were clearly decisive about it."

Petra looked at her. "Is she always like this?"

"No," Ezra said. "Usually she's quieter."

Sable gave him a look that would have frightened most people. He smiled at her.

Something in her face went soft for exactly one second before she put it back.

---

The Ariadne lifted off Gerald at 0452. Wren made no comment about the planet's unofficial name, which Ezra suspected meant she'd heard it through the hull and silently approved.

The ground dropped away. The survey outbuildings, the flat rock where Petra had been sitting when they arrived, the drill site where the fragment had been waiting — all of it went small.

Petra watched from the viewport with her hand around the stone.

"First time?" Rafe asked.

"Leaving Gerald?" she said. "Yes."

"Does it feel how you expected?"

She thought about it seriously. "I expected to feel relieved," she said. "I mostly do. But there's also—" She touched her palm to the viewport glass. "I know it's just a rock. I know Gerald is just a catalog number and the planet doesn't actually know I was there. But it kept me alive for four months, more or less, and now I'm leaving."

She watched until the planet was too small to see the outbuildings. Then she stepped back.

"Okay," she said, more to herself than anyone. "Okay."

Ezra reached into his chest pocket. He'd forgotten about it until now — mostly because it had been sitting over the star for three hours and the star had been busy.

He took out the piece of toast.

It was significantly more crumpled than it had been when Declan gave it to him. At some point during all the walking and crouching and general adventure of the last five hours, it had compressed into something that technically still resembled toast but was making its best effort.

He looked at it. He looked at Petra.

"This is from my brother," he said. "He's two. He gives toast to people who need something true and simple."

Petra looked at the toast.

"He wanted me to give it to you," Ezra said. "He doesn't know your name but he knew you were— that you'd been alone."

Petra took the toast. She held it in both hands for a moment.

"Lone-ee," she said. She must have caught the word from somewhere in the group's vocabulary, the way people did when they joined something new.

"Yeah," Ezra said. "He said that."

She ate the toast. It was terrible — old and crumbled and had been next to a cosmic energy fragment for six hours — and she ate every bit of it, and didn't make a face, and didn't say anything.

Sable looked out the viewport. Julian looked at his notebook. Ollie put his head on Petra's knee.

---

Four hours back to Monscollis.

Ezra sat with the four-note chord alive in the ship around him and tried to think about what they knew now.

Four fragments. Four holders. Gold, amber, silver-blue, iridescent-violet. Each one different. Each one chosen by the Covenant for something specific — Ezra's empathy, Rafe's attention, Sable's precision. And Petra, who had screamed at a wall and lit it up, who had named an unnamed planet Gerald, who had been alone for months with something impossible and found a way to carry it.

He wondered what Petra's power was. He wondered if she knew.

He looked at her across the cargo bay. She was asleep with Ollie half on top of her, which Ollie had apparently decided was fine. One hand still curled around the stone.

He thought about the two remaining yellow marks on Sable's chart.

And he thought about the Shadow Hand, who had a pattern reader, who knew where to look, who had two fragments sitting dark and silent and useless in whatever container they kept them in.

Four good. Two taken. One unknown.

He wondered if the unknown was still unknown to both sides.

He wondered if the Covenant was still adjusting — if the pattern reader's information was already old, if the fragments were already in new positions that nobody had predicted.

He pressed his hand to his chest. The star pulsed quiet and steady.

*Still here*, it said. *Still us.*

He closed his eyes and slept.

---

They landed at Monscollis at 0836.

The sun was up. The crystal spire caught the light and threw it in long pale lines across the clearing. Marcus radioed ahead, and by the time the Ariadne touched down, Okonkwo was there, and Mom was there, and Declan was there — held in Dad's arms because he would not have stayed anywhere else.

When the door opened, Declan pointed at the new person in the group.

"New?" he said.

"New," Ezra confirmed. "Her name is Petra."

Declan studied Petra with the focused evaluation he applied to all incoming people. He looked at her face. He looked at the pendant on its cord, which was glowing quietly in the morning light.

He pointed at it. "Pretty."

"Thank you," Petra said.

"Gerald?" he asked.

Ezra blinked. He had not mentioned Gerald. Nobody had mentioned Gerald within earshot of Declan.

Petra looked at Ezra. Ezra looked at Declan.

"How do you know about Gerald?" Ezra asked.

Declan looked at him like this was a very basic question.

"Decker know," he said, and wiggled to be put down.

Dad set him down. He walked to Petra, placed two pats on her knee, and walked back toward the main hall.

"Eggs," he announced to no one in particular, and disappeared.

Petra stared at the space where he'd been.

"He's two?" she said.

"Two," Ezra confirmed.

"Right." She looked at the crystal spire, at the glowing pendant, at the group around her — Sable and Rafe and Julian already walking toward the hall, Marcus and Okonkwo speaking quietly, Ollie trotting after Declan. "Okay." She took a breath. "This is the team, then."

"This is the team."

She adjusted the strap of her bag on her shoulder.

"Good," she said. And walked in.
