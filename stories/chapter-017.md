# Chapter 17: Something Woke Up

Three weeks after the party, things at Monscollis Colony were almost normal.

Almost.

Julian had started keeping a list called **"Things That Could Still Go Wrong."** It had fourteen items on it. Rafe had read it once and suggested adding "Julian's list itself" as item fifteen. Julian had not found that funny. Rafe still thought it was hilarious.

Ezra spent most mornings in the garden behind the cabin, practicing his elasticity. He could stretch his arm twelve feet now — two whole feet past the tingling — as long as he kept thinking about Declan and toast. It was a weird anchor, but it worked.

Declan mostly just ran around the yard saying "GO" at things.

Ollie watched all of it with calm, ancient wisdom. Or possibly he was just napping with his eyes open. Hard to tell.

---

On a Tuesday that felt perfectly unremarkable, Sable burst through the cabin door with her map tablet and a look on her face that said: *the list was right.*

"We have a problem," she said.

Julian sat up straight. "Which item?"

Sable blinked. "What?"

"On the list. Which item number?"

"There's no list number, Julian. There's a signal."

She held up the tablet. In the center of the glowing map of their solar system, a small yellow dot pulsed. It was at the edge of the Outer Ring — far past Meridian Point, past the last colony outpost, past the invisible line on old star charts that just said *unmapped.*

Ezra leaned in close. "That looks like a fragment."

"It is," Sable said. "Fragment Six."

Rafe set down his mug of morning tea. "I thought the Shadow Hand had Fragment Six."

"They did," Sable said. "Until three days ago, when whatever was holding it... stopped holding it."

The room went very quiet.

Declan looked up from the floor, where he had been building a tower out of spoons. "Uh-oh," he said.

Everyone stared at him.

"He's right," Petra said. "Uh-oh."

---

Sable spread the map out on the kitchen table. She pointed to a small cluster of moons on the far edge of the Outer Ring.

"This is a place called the Shale Drift," she said. "Nobody goes there. It's mostly fog and old rock formations."

"Why was the Shadow Hand there?" Julian asked.

"They weren't. Not the Shadow Hand we fought, anyway." Sable paused. "I think this signal is coming from a *splinter group.* A second branch we didn't know about."

Noa, who had been leaning in the doorway eating a piece of bread, stopped chewing. "There's another Shadow Hand?"

"Maybe. Or something that used to work *with* the Shadow Hand."

"That's worse," Noa said.

"Probably," Sable agreed.

Julian had already opened his notebook. He was writing very fast.

---

They left for the Shale Drift the next morning on Captain Okonkwo's old scout ship. It was smaller than their usual transport and smelled like engine grease and old sandwiches.

"I like it," Declan announced, patting the wall.

"Of course you do," Julian muttered.

The trip took six hours. Noa and Rafe played cards in the back. Petra sat by the window with her fragment in her palm, watching the stars blur past. Ezra fell asleep against Ollie, which meant Ollie was also technically required to stay still for the whole trip, which Ollie handled with quiet dignity.

Sable flew the ship.

Julian sat in the co-pilot seat with his notebook and tried to plan for every possible thing that could happen in the Shale Drift. He got to page four before Sable said, "You should sleep."

"I'm strategizing."

"You're catastrophizing. Sleep."

Julian closed the notebook. He did not sleep. But he did stare at the ceiling quietly for two hours, which was almost the same thing.

---

The Shale Drift looked like the inside of a snow globe, except instead of snow it was grey rock dust, and instead of a tiny Christmas village at the bottom it was a jagged black cliff that nobody wanted to land on.

"That's the landing site," Sable said, pointing directly at the jagged black cliff.

"Of course it is," Petra said.

The scout ship bumped down onto a ledge just wide enough for the landing gear. Outside, the fog rolled in slow, heavy waves. The fragment signal on Sable's tablet pulsed steady and close.

"It's underground," Sable said. "There's a passage in the cliff face. Maybe fifty meters down."

"Ollie," Ezra said quietly.

Ollie had been sitting by the hatch door. His ears were forward. His grey curly fur was standing up along his spine.

"He senses something," Ezra said.

"Danger?" Julian asked.

"I don't know. Something old."

Declan toddled over and put his hand flat on the hatch door. He stood very still.

"Hello," he said softly.

Nobody was sure who he was talking to.

---

The passage in the cliff was narrow and smelled like wet stone. Petra made a small light with her palm — not a shield this time, just a warm glow, like a lantern — and led the way.

Rafe kept his hands loose and ready. Julian counted his steps out of habit. Noa's headphones were around her neck instead of on it, which Ezra had figured out was her version of *paying close attention.*

The passage opened up after thirty meters.

It opened into a cave the size of a gymnasium. The walls were covered in old carvings — spirals and lines and shapes that Ezra didn't recognize. In the very center of the cave floor, resting in a small hollow of rock, was a fragment.

It was dark red, almost brown. Like dried rust. Or old blood.

It was not glowing.

"That's not right," Sable said quietly. "Fragments always glow."

"Unless they've been drained," Rafe said.

Sable nodded slowly. "Yes. Unless that."

Julian crouched down near the fragment without touching it. "Drained by what?"

Nobody answered, because at that exact moment, Ollie started barking.

---

It came from the far wall of the cave.

A shape. Tall. Made of something that wasn't quite shadow and wasn't quite fog — a deep, shifting grey that moved like smoke but had *edges.* Two very faint lights where eyes might be.

Ezra stepped in front of Declan without thinking.

The shape did not move.

It just looked at them.

Then it made a sound. Not words. Not a roar. Something lower and older than both — a hum that seemed to come from the walls themselves, the carvings, the rock, the air.

Declan made the same sound back.

Perfectly.

Exact pitch, exact length, exact rumble.

The shape went completely still.

Sable whispered: "Declan. Don't—"

"Hi," Declan said to the shape. "I'm Decker."

The shape made another sound. Shorter this time. Almost like a question.

Declan nodded seriously. "Uh-huh."

Ezra had no idea what any of that meant, but the shape slowly — slowly — stepped back two feet. Not leaving. Not attacking. Just... making room.

Petra let out a breath. "What just happened?"

"I think Declan said something polite," Noa said.

"In ancient cave-creature," Rafe added.

"He's two," Julian said, like that explained it, even though it explained absolutely nothing.

---

They didn't take the fragment that day.

Sable said they needed to learn more about what the shape was before they touched anything it was guarding. Julian wrote six pages of notes. Petra sketched the wall carvings from memory. Rafe catalogued every object in the cave without moving a single one.

Noa stood next to Declan on the way back to the ship. "Do you know what that thing was?" she asked.

Declan thought about it with great seriousness.

"Old friend," he said.

Noa looked at Ezra.

Ezra shrugged. "He said 'Gerald' before we went to Gerald. I stopped asking questions."

They loaded back onto the ship. The fog closed in behind them as they lifted off.

Far below, in the cave, the dark-red fragment pulsed once.

It was dim. But it was there.

Something was waking up — and for the first time since the Shadow Hand, Ezra wasn't sure if that was good news or bad news.

He held his gold fragment gently in his palm.

It was warm.

That was enough, for now.
